## **THE NATURAL FLOW (FOLLOWS CANONICAL HISTORY)**

### **STEP 1: HEBREW ORIGINALS (1400-400 BC)**

**Hebrew Scriptures written:**
- Torah (1400-1200 BC)
- Prophets (800-400 BC)
- Writings (1000-400 BC)

**Language:** Biblical Hebrew (paleo-Hebrew script, later Aramaic square script)

**Texts:** What later became the Masoretic Text tradition

**This is the SOURCE. All else flows FROM here.**

---

### **STEP 2: GREEK SEPTUAGINT (3rd-2nd Century BC)**

**Historical reality:**

**282-246 BC (traditional date):** Jewish scholars in Alexandria translate Torah into Greek for Diaspora Jews who no longer read Hebrew fluently.

**Why this happened:**
- Alexander's conquests (333 BC) → Greek becomes lingua franca
- Jewish Diaspora in Egypt/Mediterranean loses Hebrew literacy
- Synagogues need Scripture in Greek for worship/study

**Result:** Septuagint (LXX) becomes THE Scripture for Greek-speaking Jews

**Evidence:**
- **Philo of Alexandria (20 BC - 50 AD):** Quotes LXX exclusively, never Hebrew
- **Josephus (37-100 AD):** Uses LXX for biblical references
- **Dead Sea Scrolls:** Some Hebrew texts align more with LXX than later MT (proving LXX reflects real Hebrew variants, not just interpretation)

---

### **STEP 3: NEW TESTAMENT AUTHORS QUOTE LXX (50-95 AD)**

**This is the KEY observation:**

**NT authors quote Greek OT, not Hebrew OT.**

**Statistics:**
- **300+ OT quotations in NT**
- **~90% match LXX wording** (not MT)
- **~10%** follow MT or mixed tradition

**Examples:**

#### **Example 1: Habakkuk 2:4 → Romans 1:17**

**Hebrew MT:**
> וְצַדִּיק בֶּאֱמוּנָתוֹ יִחְיֶה
> "But the righteous by his faithfulness shall live"

**Greek LXX:**
> ὁ δὲ δίκαιος ἐκ πίστεώς μου ζήσεται
> "But the righteous by my [God's] faithfulness shall live"

**Paul in Romans 1:17:**
> ὁ δὲ δίκαιος ἐκ πίστεως ζήσεται
> "The righteous by faithfulness shall live"

**Paul follows LXX structure (ἐκ πίστεως), not MT (בֶּאֱמוּנָתוֹ).**

**This isn't random. Paul READ the LXX. This was his Bible.**

---

#### **Example 2: Isaiah 7:14 → Matthew 1:23**

**Hebrew MT:**
> הִנֵּה הָעַלְמָה הָרָה וְיֹלֶדֶת בֵּן
> "Behold, the young woman shall conceive and bear a son"

**Greek LXX:**
> ἰδοὺ ἡ παρθένος ἐν γαστρὶ ἕξει καὶ τέξεται υἱόν
> "Behold, the virgin shall be pregnant and bear a son"

**Matthew 1:23:**
> ἰδοὺ ἡ παρθένος ἐν γαστρὶ ἕξει καὶ τέξεται υἱόν
> (Identical to LXX)

**Matthew quotes LXX verbatim, including παρθένος (virgin) instead of עַלְמָה (young woman).**

**This shows Matthew's BIBLE was Greek, not Hebrew.**

#### **Example 3: Psalm 22:16 (22:17 MT) → John 19:37**

**Hebrew MT:**
> כָּאֲרִי יָדַי וְרַגְלָי
> "Like a lion, my hands and my feet"

**Greek LXX:**

**John 19:37 (fulfillment note):**
> ὄψονται εἰς ὃν ἐξεκέντησαν
> "They will look on him whom they pierced"

**John assumes piercing (LXX reading), not mauling (MT reading).**

**Again: LXX is the biblical text the NT uses.**

### **STEP 4: LATIN VULGATE (382-405 AD)**

**Jerome's translation:**

**Two-stage process:**
1. **Old Latin (Vetus Latina, 2nd-4th cent):** Translated FROM Greek LXX
2. **Jerome's Vulgate (382-405 AD):** Translated FROM Hebrew (OT) and Greek (NT), but consulted LXX

**Why Jerome matters:**

**He's the first major Western translator to go back to Hebrew for OT.**

**Before Jerome:**
- Western church used Old Latin (= LXX in Latin)
- Greek LXX was considered authoritative

**Jerome's innovation:**
- Translate OT from "Hebraica veritas" (Hebrew truth)
- BUT he still consulted LXX when Hebrew was ambiguous

**Result:** Vulgate becomes Western church's Bible for 1,000+ years

## **POLYSEMY PRESERVATION EXAMPLE**

### **רוּחַ (ruach) → πνεῦμα (pneuma) → spiritus**

**Hebrew רוּחַ (ruach) has 3 meanings:**
1. Wind (physical)
2. Breath (biological)
3. Spirit (divine/personal)

**Examples:**
- **Wind:** Exodus 10:13 - "YHWH brought east wind (רוּחַ)"
- **Breath:** Job 27:3 - "Spirit (רוּחַ) of God in my nostrils"
- **Spirit:** Genesis 1:2 - "Spirit (רוּחַ) of God hovering"

**Greek πνεῦμα preserves all 3:**
- **Wind:** John 3:8 - "πνεῦμα blows where it wishes"
- **Breath:** 2 Thess 2:8 - "breath (πνεῦμα) of his mouth"
- **Spirit:** Acts 2:4 - "filled with Holy Spirit (πνεῦμα)"

**Latin spiritus ALSO preserves all 3:**
- **Wind:** "spiritus vehemens" (violent wind)
- **Breath:** "spiritus vitae" (breath of life)
- **Spirit:** "Spiritus Sanctus" (Holy Spirit)

**This polysemy flows naturally Hebrew → Greek → Latin.**

**English breaks it:**
- "Wind" (loses spiritual sense)
- "Breath" (loses wind sense)
- "Spirit" (loses physical senses)
- "Ghost" (total departure)
