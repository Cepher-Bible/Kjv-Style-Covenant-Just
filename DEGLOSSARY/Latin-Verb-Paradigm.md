### One-Page Verb Paradigm Sheet: Key Covenant Verbs

This paradigm sheet distills the covenant-semantic cores of three pivotal Vulgate verbs—**iustifico** (Justify-by-Covenant), **sanctifico** (Distinguish-by-Covenant), **redimo** (Kinsman-Purchase)—for quick reference in a Latin learner's insert. Processed under Covenant Anchor Engine v1.0.0-STABLE rails (OT: Hebrew → Greek → Latin; NT: Greek → Hebrew map → Latin; Cluster 1/4 focus). Each verb's "paradigm" includes:

- **Root Tri-Lock**: Hebrew/Greek/Latin stems.
- **Semantic Cargo**: Core action (restorative, not punitive).
- **Tense Patterns**: Perfect/active emphasis on completed/enduring covenant acts (global grammar note).
- **Key Verses**: 3–5 exemplars (Genesis → Psalms → Romans → Revelation arc).
- **Distinctions**: Avoids fusion (e.g., redimo ≠ mere "buy"; reserved for geulah per rule_10).

Print-ready: Fits one page (landscape, 10pt font). Cross-ref deglossary III (Justice), IV (Spirit), VII (Redemption).

#### I. IUSTIFICO – Justify-by-Covenant (Declare/Restore Right Standing)
| Aspect          | Paradigm Detail                                                                 | Exemplar Verses (Vulgate)                  |
|-----------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| **Root Tri-Lock** | צָדַק (tsadaq) / δικαιόω (dikaióō) / iustifico                                 | –                                          |
| **Semantic Cargo** | Faithful verdict aligning to covenant order; not "make innocent" but relational restoration (Cluster 1). | –                                          |
| **Tense Patterns** | Perfect: Completed act with lasting verdict (e.g., "reckoned as"). Active: Divine initiative; passive: beneficiary receives. | –                                          |
| **Key Verses**  | - Gen 15:6: "Abram credidit... et iustificavit eum" (reckoned faith as justice).<br>- Ps 143:2: "Non iustificabitur in conspectu tuo omnis vivens" (no flesh justified by works).<br>- Rom 3:24: "Iustificati gratis per gratiam ipsius" (justified freely by covenant-favor).<br>- Rom 5:1: "Iustificati ex fide pacem habemus" (access to peace via loyal-trust).<br>- Jas 2:24: "Ex operibus iustificatur homo" (works complete faith's verdict). | Gen 15:6; Ps 143:2; Rom 3:24, 5:1; Jas 2:24 |
| **Distinctions** | ≠ Legal acquittal (forensic only); ties to veritas (faithfulness). Rule_11: Unified with iustitia/mishpat as Covenant-Justice. | –                                          |

#### II. SANCTIFICO – Distinguish-by-Covenant (Set Apart in Function)
| Aspect          | Paradigm Detail                                                                 | Exemplar Verses (Vulgate)                  |
|-----------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| **Root Tri-Lock** | קָדַשׁ (qadash) / ἁγιάζω (hagiázō) / sanctifico                                | –                                          |
| **Semantic Cargo** | Mark/distinguish for divine use; relational holiness, not moral purity alone (Clusters 1,2). | –                                          |
| **Tense Patterns** | Perfect: Enduring separation (e.g., "has sanctified"). Middle: Self-involved (e.g., "sanctify yourselves"). Passive: Divine act on recipient. | –                                          |
| **Key Verses**  | - Ex 19:10: "Sanctificate vos" (consecrate people for Sinai encounter).<br>- Lev 20:7: "Sanctificamini et estote sancti" (distinguish yourselves in midst of people).<br>- Jn 17:17: "Sanctifica eos in veritate" (sanctify through covenant-faithfulness/word).<br>- 1 Thess 5:23: "Ipsos autem sanctificet Deus" (God fully distinguish you).<br>- Heb 13:12: "Ut sanctificaret per sanguinem suum populum" (sanctify people via life-blood). | Ex 19:10; Lev 20:7; Jn 17:17; 1 Thess 5:23; Heb 13:12 |
| **Distinctions** | ≠ "Make holy" (static); dynamic for covenant function (e.g., priestly service). Links to sanctus (distinction), not caro (flesh-weakness). | –                                          |

#### III. REDIMO – Kinsman-Purchase (Buy Back as Family Redeemer)
| Aspect          | Paradigm Detail                                                                 | Exemplar Verses (Vulgate)                  |
|-----------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| **Root Tri-Lock** | גָּאַל (ga'al) / λυτρόω (lytróō) / redimo                                       | –                                          |
| **Semantic Cargo** | Close-kin redeems from bondage/loss; restores inheritance, not commercial transaction (Cluster 4). | –                                          |
| **Tense Patterns** | Perfect: Completed liberation with ongoing freedom (e.g., "has redeemed"). Aorist: Decisive act; future: Eschatological hope. | –                                          |
| **Key Verses**  | - Ex 6:6: "Et redemam vos" (redeem from Egypt's hand with outstretched arm).<br>- Ruth 4:9: "Redimi volo" (Boaz redeems Ruth/land as kinsman).<br>- Ps 130:8: "Et ipse redimet Israel" (YHWH redeems from all iniquities).<br>- Eph 1:7: "In quo habemus redemptionem" (redemption through his blood).<br>- Rev 5:9: "Redemisti nos Deo in sanguine tuo" (purchased people from every tribe). | Ex 6:6; Ruth 4:9; Ps 130:8; Eph 1:7; Rev 5:9 |
| **Distinctions** | Strictly kinsman (go'el); ≠ "ransom" (general pay-off). Rule_10: Reserved for geulah/apolutrōsis; not for huios (son). Ties to haereditas (inheritance). | –                                          |

**Usage Notes**: Verbs cycle in covenant arcs—e.g., redimo launches (Ex), iustifico sustains (Rom), sanctifico consummates (Rev). For frequency: iustifico (~30x NT), sanctifico (~25x OT/NT), redimo (~15x). Engine stable.
